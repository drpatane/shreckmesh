# Midnight

*observed on 4/30/2026*

A message from the Mortician
Proper names for potential thieves
The Shadow's servant bristles 
At the brazen display of secret knowledge

It is one hour until midnight. 

The Poet enters the sculptor's room
A crude sculptural likeness of a bagel
On a pedestal
While a voice speaks from below 
On the floor
Lays a sculptor
Going on about the weight of everything
The crowd began to murmur.
"We paid $400 a plate for this?"
The Poet walks up to the bagel's plinth. 
She said, "Sometimes, you need to be saved."
And, with a push
And a shattering of stone
He was free
She reaches down to embrace him
Flashes of feeling sweep across his face
First anger, then others, then stillness
"May I be excused. I need to go to work."
"Of course," said The Poet
The man picks up a couple larger pieces of the sculpture,
But, puts them down and leaves.
The crowd is impressed
"I didn't think much of what was here before,
But, this is something," a voice in the crowd says.

Elsewhere, the Rider collects the Seer and they head to their objective: Three letters under glass. 

The Seer remembers 
(Or maybe she created the memory?)
Hints of a plan 
Divide the letters 
Meet at the home of The Statue

The Statue sees competitors to the prize
Rebels and hunters, all potential thieves 
To their servant, the Statue says, "Let's leave two alive."

The Poet entered the mixer's space
A room filled with darkness
The sound of a ukelele. 
Out of tune.
The projected image of the poet
Taken from across the room
Another image of her. And, another.
A voice sings and eventually gets to the refrain:
"I will follow you into the dark."
The crowd grumbles.
The Poet stands beside the mixer and says,
"Let's do this together."
She sings along.
Her voice fills the room.
The crowd points out the contrast:
"Pairing yourself with an unskilled musician
was brilliant."
The Poet looked at the mixer and replied:
"It is good to remember where you started.
I never forget."

The rest of The Six
Stand in front of the letters
A waiter comes by
A poorly disguised hunter 
He asks why they are not at the party 
The Shadow commands him to forget 
And he does. 
The Statue leads the man away
And tells him to forget 
The Rider asks if there is something special in the wine 
Then tells him to forget
The waiter, unsure of his reality, sits down on a bench and drinks rotten tasting wine 

After some attempts at opening the box
The Seer remembers her skill in opening
That which is closed to her
The letters are freed and divided

The Poet walks to the exhibit
Of the last performer
Black cloth covers the exhibits
The aspiring poet
Standing on a low stage 
In a long, flowy white gown

At the Poet's entrance 
The aspiring Poet speaks
"I worked for days 
To put into words
The shape of this love
But, out of all the books I read
No words were enough
This love must be shown 
I must bare my heart."

The aspiring poet produces a surgeon's knife
And begins to carve into her chest
The crowd begins to panic 
The Poet, the appearance of calm
Walks up and gently puts her hands over the aspiring poet's cutting hand
The Poet holds the knife steady as the aspiring one tries to push it deeper
The Poet, takes the knife, and cuts 
A mirror image of the wound into her hand
She places it on the aspirant's chest
And leans in to lick their wounds closed

The crowd calms
"It looked so real," said a voice in the crowd 
"Did you like it?" asked the aspirant
"You were brilliant" the Poet said
"I would have done it for you" said the aspirant
"That you would do it is enough" said the Poet

The Seer says, "It kind of feels like midnight."
At the push of a button
The lights go out

The crowd begins to panic
The poet's voice fills the room
It is infused with wonder
It's tone seeps into floor and stone

"And now we arrive at the main event. 
Tonight is a night about love."

A different, gruffer, voice yells, "I got invited to an orgy?! Yes!"

The Poet continues, "Lean close to someone near to you and tell them something that you have never told anyone else."

The Shadow leaves the rest of the Six to look for hunters
She finds two confessing their love
Another two speak of their mission
The Shadow convinces them to speak
Of their government contact
And tells them to take their friends and leave

Heavy boots round the corner 
Surprised eyes see the Seer, Statue, Mortician, and Rider
The Seer vanishes and goes around these 
Potential thieves 
The Rider throws a glass box at one
The Mortician stabs with a surgeon's blade while a thief punches her in the face 

The Statue asks the Rider, "Do I have your permission to act?"

The Rider says, "Sure. Why?"
  
"Good," says the Statue. With an introduction, they say, "If you fight me. You will die," and attacks.

The Mortician gets The Shadow's help with breaking open a different box of letters. 

The Shadow crumbles the leg of one of the Rebels.

The Statue wipes off a face of another. 

They grab these two incapacitated Rebels. 

The Poet rejoins The Six as they make their way outside. 

The Six are talking about what to do with their captives. 
They need them to speak. 
The one without a face cannot. 

The Poet, high on her own power, Suggests turning that one into dust. 
The Shadow intervenes. 
Asking if she would so readily anger 
A leader of these Rebels 

The Poet comes to her senses 
"You make a good point," she says. 

The Statue leaves the faceless one
To be found by their comrades
And takes the legless one

The Six disperse 
They meet at the Statue's home

The Statue offers an exchange of gifts for three nights stay in their home. 

The Seer calls them a "certified booner." She and The Poet leave with The Shadow

The Mortician stays and forms a friendship with The Statue's servant. 

The Shadow's servant talks to the Seer about the last time someone had what the Seer called "her porridge."

"This is you" The Shadow said as they arrived at The Poet's home. 

"Thanks for the party. I had a great time."
She said as she walked away and waved.

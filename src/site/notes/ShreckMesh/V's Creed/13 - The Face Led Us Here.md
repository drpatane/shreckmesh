# The Face Led Us Here

*observed on 5/28/2026*

At the house of The Statue
Stood The Six
Or, there were six
Now only Four 
The Shadow and servant were gone
The Seer had walked out
Scrolling the feed

The Four conferred 
To choose a loose end to follow
Confront the witch? 
Attend the family reunion? 
Or visit the house of a hunter? 

The witch was the Shadow's prize
And, the family reunion would be fraught 
Without the Shadow's presence

Decision made
Statue, Rider, Poet, and Mortician 
Set their sight north to the wooded land

The Statue called their servant
Who asked which autocarriage to ride
Stately or speedy?
Expedience was chosen
The Rider had a question 
"Mind if I take my bike?"
"I don't mind" said the Poet

The Shadow's servant 
Brought around a purring machine
Power in the guise of luxury

"How fast does this thing go?"
Asked the Rider
"The meter goes to 150. I don't know."
Said the servant
"Want to find out?"
The Rider asked

The carriage sped forward
Almost leaping onto the highway

The Statue held on
The Poet squealed in glee
The Mortician closed her eyes

The Rider allowed a head start
But the distance grew faster than
She had thought it would
The Rider rocketed forward 
She soon zoomed past
Quickly taking and expanding her lead. 

The servant waxed lyrical
"Hot damn. I need to find out 
What engine she's got
In that thing.
I mean,
That vehicle."

Flashing blue and yellow lights
A siren's rapid whining
A police cruiser gave chase
A tilt of her helmet
The Rider sped forward  
And a California redwood 
Stories tall
Fell across the highway
As the Rider sped under it

The police cruiser swerved
Flipping bottom over top
Over and over

"Are we going to stop?!"
Asked the Poet in alarm
The Statue's servant 
Slammed the brakes
And stopped inches from the tree

The Statue calmly spoke
"Now, now, the tree is not there
Go through it"

The Statue's servant slowly approached 
The car passed through

Minutes later 
The Four are reunited
At the house in a wooded land
Of which the face had spoken 

The Rider was already there 
Houses identical
In an Eastern European style 
Golf carts and children's toys

This house had no cars and no toys
The Rider looked in the mailbox
Letters from banks
A thick letter from lawyers
A finalization of divorce
A split of assets
Two children to the mother

"Why are we here?"
The Statue asked
The Poet said
"These guys attacked us
And we are checking out this place
That the Mortician heard from a face.
One of their faces. 
You both have a thing for faces."

The Statue suggested the back door
The party obliged
A pool, green with neglect
A light turns on with the party's motion

The back door is locked
But, the Driver kicked it in
And it opens to a dark kitchen
With a rack holding two pans 
A rotten smell greets them.

A hallway ahead
Rooms to the left and right
Stairs going up 
A door under the stairs

The Poet goes left. 
The Mortician goes right
To the left
A room mostly empty 
Indents in the carpet 
Where heavier things 
Used to be
Now only a recliner
A coffee table
A huge TV 
A glinting object on 
A built in bookshelf
"We believe in family"
A multi picture frame
Most pictures missing 
A picture torn in half
A young white man wears a tuxedo
Another frame has 6 or 7 year old children

"Pathetic" said the Statue
The Rider opens the door under the stairs
"I think I found the murder basement" she says
"I don't smell murder" said the Mortician
"It's hyperbole" said the Rider
"Let us know if the find any murder"
Said the Poet

The Mortician stands in an office
A safe looks new 
She soon finds
A manual with the default code
She tries it and the safe opens 

Inside
A plastic bottle with a painted gold cross 
It's a vial of holy water 
An FBI badge 
The white man in the tuxedo in a photo not torn 
A woman with short platinum blonde hair 
A letter clipped to the photo 
Typed out begging to be taken back 
Apologies for past infidelities 
This new job will make her proud 

Another letter in an FBI letterhead 
Recognition of faith-based anti-crime community organization 

The Rider goes down the stairs to the basement 
She clicks on the light 
Strips of carpet scraps 
A bench press and a squat rack 
A pegboard with a map and red string 
A rifle rack filled with assault rifles and shotguns 
Various versions 
Of progressively improving 
Sharpened stakes 
Cabinets and a counter with clothes on it 
A small statue of the Virgin Mary in a corner 

The Statue asks their servant 
to look at the weapons
A lot of money was spent 
On weapons assembled 
Extravagantly and incorrectly 
"You may take what you like"
The Statue said
The servant picks up a rifle and says 
"I have not used one of these since the war" as he walks out

The writer looks at the Virgin Mary 
A mix of the European figure and the Virgin de Guadalupe.
She picks up the figurine 
and raises it to smash on the floor 

A click 
A flash
White fire everywhere
A call to go mad
Quickly resisted
White hot chunks
In the Rider's body

The Statue pulls out the burning pieces
As they close the wounds 
With a pinch of fingers on flesh

A fire grows 
Up the wall
Toward the map

The Poet empties
A bucket of water near the map
To slow its spread
She takes a picture
Of the map with her camera

Everyone leaves except the Statue 
Who goes upstairs
And sees a children's room
Brand new toys on the bed
The master bedroom a mess 
A journal grabbed from a nightstand
The Statue emerges from the back window
Like a spider
Growing long to touch the ground

Back at the Statue's home
The journal is read 
It is the story of a man 
Descending into bitterness 
Gloating about an affair 
A break of a few months 
Being discovered 
The divorce being filed 
Losing the kids 
Her taking everything 
Desires for revenge 
Sitting and reading the Bible for the first time 
Returning to religion 
Returning to church 
Seeing some kind of shadow person 
Thought they were stories 
But, no, they are real 
Feeling called by God to rid the world of creatures 
A difficult first hunt 
The slow torture and murder
Of what could be an innocent 
The burning of a body
He thinks he killed a blood sucker 
One of the last entries 
A plan to meet with 
The Order of the Holy Virgin

While planning the next job 
Was visited out of the blue 
By a man and a black suit 

The poet sends the picture of the map 
None of the homes of the six are marked 
Other places marked for days yet to come 
Printouts from weird news sites 
And conspiracy forums
An article describing parasites 
Destroying the modern family 

One address in downtown 
Of a federal building 

The Mortician looks at letters taken from the safe 
It is printed on decent paper 

The FBI badge is flimsy 
Printed on printer paper 

The Mortician considers 
Sharing information 
With the rebels

The Poet holds the letter 
"I have a friend 
that works at a shop 
that sells nice paper"
she says 

The Rider lays on the floor 
Horribly hurt 
Wanting to rest

The Statue checks on her guests 

The Statue's servant
Tie used as a blindfold
Assembles and disassembles a rifle 
"Master, sire, I feel like we will need more of these soon" he says

# This Masquerade

*observed on 4/16/2026*

The six returned a cub to its lair 
And woke him up
The Poet sitting on his chest
The Rider's boot upon his neck 
They pulled words from his lips
He spoke
Of a woman with raven hair
Falling to her hips

He had met her at a bar 
The oldest in the city
The six knew of the place
And let him go 
No time to waste

The lair's boss, 
But a kid with dirty bare feet
Could not care less
Except for a ruffling of hair 
On the head of his cub
And left with a howl into the night 

The Shadow sent word to the six
A map of the museum 
With hastily drawn symbols
Perfect for the Seer's symbolic sight 

The Poet surveys her preparations
Places for her performers 
Sensory delights
For those who wander
This masquerade 

Her prime performers
A sculptor, a mixer, a poet
Squabble over the central place
Trying to claim the short circular stage
The Poet stops the spat
Saying, "The center is mine"

They acquiesce in adoration 
The sculptor sheds a tear 
She walks them to their stations 
The sculptor kneels in supplication 
With a hand on his bowed head
She says
"The tears are good. 
You should use them."
"Thank you, my muse!"
He says with a hand outstretched
The Poet walks away

The Statue stands
Resplendent 
Observing 
As the guests from her list arrive
The wealthy who wield 
The belief that they know
As much about art
As the artifice
Of their fragile lives

The Rider arrives with golden vessels 
Meant for the guests 
To powder themselves
As they recline in the restrooms 

The Mortician produces from a coffin
Bottles of wine
Decades old vintages
Some more viscous than others
And has a helper place ambrosia
On a table

The Seer appears in what she calls
"A leather onesie" 
With a scintillating zipper
And a cat mask

The guests begin to arrive 
As they fill the room
The Poet stands on the center stage
Arms open by her sides

She greets them with words 
She pulls from the air:

"Forget the world you have left before
This place you enter now
Is where you will know
A love you've never known
Or will ever know since"

"Give in to your senses
See the delights before you
Listen your heart beat inside of your chest 
Feel the core of your being
Give in to love"

The Mortician left the room
And told the Six of three letters 
Under alarmed glass
And other eyes interested 
In stealing them too
She read the placard
And saw the names
Rachel and Georgios 
Ancient Greek on Vellum 
Latin on Parchment
English on Paper
Three letters written in blood

# Three Observations

*observed on 2/18/2026*

*Editor's note: We hope our anonymous poet forgives the title change. We must maintain some kind of organization here, even in the midst of artistic mania.*
# Who I Would Be

She thinks she is so cool
She thinks she is special 
She is above it all
So I made her like me
I made her see
What makes me cool
What makes me special 
What makes me above it all
But I still can't shake it
She is who I would be
If I was free

# A Phantom Face

A face
A knife
A cross
And candles
A circle
A woman
The Mortician 
Cuts her palm
Drips the life
The sound of a heart
Deep it beats
The cross drinks
The face moves
The face speaks
"Knight"
"Order"
"The Holy Virgin"
Do you know the man in black?
Asked the Poet
"The Man"
"Came to us"
"Found us"
Where?
The Shadow asked
"House"
Address?
The face said the name 
Of a crossing 
"We will destroy you"
Not before we destroy you first
The Rider said and crushed the cross
NOOO 
Said the face as the voice faded away

# We Thought We Killed Them All

The Rider asked for help
The Five came to her aid
A gathering place for wanderers
A jolly doorman named Crankshaft 
Other riders 
A hushed conversation in the back
With one the Rider wished to protect
A patch shown with a lion on fire
A cross in its mouth
The Sons of Cain
The Rider said
"We thought wehad killed them all."

The sound of broken glass
Crankshaft laying out
across the window's frame

The Shadow lunged at the culprit 
Like a spear
The sound of a broken back

Three others hold bars of iron
Two others point firearms 

The Poet calls 
"I need to talk to you "
One of the gunmen, Harry
Walks to her
She pours him a drink 

The Seer punches one in the head
"What are you doing old lady?"
"Sorry!" The Seer says

The Rider breaks a bottle 
And stands at the doorway
In front of a man with an iron bludgeon
The Mortician slices at his neck

The Shadow kicks one in the nethers
Pop!
He curls up on the ground like a grub 
Do you still want to go?
The Shadow asks
One runs

The Rider takes off a head
With a broken bottle and kicks it
At the running man
The man runs faster 

The Poet talks to the man at the bar
He works for a man named Bear
He is big
He is hairy 
He pays these men

The Rider gets on her bike 
To chase the man down
But her head is slammed into the ground 
By a big, hairy hand 
Of a big, hairy man
